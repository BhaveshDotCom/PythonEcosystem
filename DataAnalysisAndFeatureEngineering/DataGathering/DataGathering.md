# Data Analysis Process

Data analysis is the process of collecting, cleaning, studying, and presenting data to find useful information. It usually follows these five steps:

## 1. Asking Questions
The first step is to decide what problem you want to solve or what information you need. A clear question helps guide the entire analysis.

**Example:**
- Why are sales decreasing?
- Which product is the most popular?

---

## 2. Data Wrangling
Data is often incomplete or messy. In this step, the data is cleaned and prepared by:
- Removing duplicate records
- Handling missing values
- Correcting errors
- Organizing data into a proper format

This ensures the data is ready for analysis.

---

## 3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
EDA is used to understand the data by finding patterns, trends, and relationships.

Common activities include:
- Calculating averages and totals
- Creating charts and graphs
- Finding outliers
- Comparing different groups

This step helps discover useful insights.

---

## 4. Drawing Conclusions
After analyzing the data, conclusions are made based on the findings.

**Example:**
- Sales are lower because demand decreases during the rainy season.
- Customers aged 18–25 buy the product the most.

The conclusions should always be supported by the data.

---

## 5. Communicating Results
The final step is to present the findings in a simple and understandable way.

Results can be shared using:
- Reports
- Charts and graphs
- Dashboards
- Presentations
